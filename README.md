# AI Fact Check: The Social Ontology of Madness

This repository documents an AI-assisted fact-checking workflow analyzing a video essay on the social construction of psychiatric diagnosis. The project moves from raw transcription to deep research, drafting, and finally typesetting a professional report in both English and German using [Typst](https://typst.app/).

## üîç Main Conclusions

The review panel (simulated expert persona) found the video's central thesis to be **highly robust** and supported by extensive medical scholarship.

*   **Historical Accuracy:** Claims regarding obsolete diagnoses like *Nostalgia* (fatal homesickness), *Lactational Insanity*, and *Drapetomania* are historically accurate; these were treated as objective medical realities in their respective eras.
*   **Sociological Context:** The video accurately describes the class stratification of WWI psychiatry (Officers diagnosed with "Neurasthenia" vs. enlisted men with "Hysteria").
*   **Neurobiology:** Claims regarding synaptic pruning deficits in Autism align with current leading scientific theories (e.g., Tang et al., 2014).
*   **Verdict:** The video successfully demonstrates that psychiatric diagnoses are historically contingent constructs rather than immutable biological natural kinds.
